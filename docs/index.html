Welcome to SecretMag!

The only magazine where you can get to know more about the secret organizations.

Uncover the secrets with "SecretMag"!

Explore the secret organizations' world by diving into the three issues, each unveiling the mysteries of a society through a trio of compelling articles.

"SecretMag" is more than a simple magazine; it is based on MMMM: Mixing Maps, Magazines, and Metadata.

  • Mixing: immerse yourself in a unique framework of tools that brings textual and multimedia articles to life, presented in a magazine-style layout. The pages come alive with sophisticated designs and a graphical rendering of geographical features.
  • Magazines: delve into curated issues — KKK, Freemasonry, and Skull and Bones — each a captivating exploration of hidden worlds.
  • Maps: navigate seamlessly within each article using an interactive map integrated into the metadata sidebar.
  • Metadata: uncover the stories behind people, dates, events, and places as each article is tagged.

Ready to unveil the secrets? Start your journey now and learn more about the issues!

Issue 1: KKK

The Ku Klux Klan (KKK), established in 1865 in Pulaski, Tennessee, is a notorious white supremacist organization that has consistently promoted white supremacy, racism, and the preservation of what it considers traditional American values. Employing tactics of intimidation, violence, and terrorism, the KKK has targeted African Americans, minority groups, immigrants, Catholics, Jews, and others perceived as threats to their vision of a white-dominated society.

Issue 2: Freemasonry

Freemasonry, a fraternal organization, traces its roots to the early 17th century in Europe, although its origins are shrouded in mystery. It was formally established with the founding of the United Grand Lodge of England in 1717. Freemasonry is built upon a set of core values, including brotherhood, morality, and community service. Members, known as Masons, are encouraged to cultivate personal growth, ethical conduct, and a commitment to the welfare of others. The organization employs a system of rituals and symbolism to impart these values, emphasizing the importance of mutual support and tolerance among its members.

Issue 3: Skull and Bones

The Skull and Bones is a highly secretive and exclusive collegiate secret society founded at Yale University in 1832. Its origins and activities are shrouded in mystery, and members are sworn to secrecy about their activities. The society is known for its selectivity, with only 15 undergraduate members, known as Bonesmen, being admitted each year. The values and objectives of Skull and Bones remain closely guarded secrets, but it is believed to emphasize personal and professional success, as many of its alumni have gone on to occupy influential positions in various fields. Despite the intrigue surrounding it, the society's secretive nature has given rise to numerous conspiracy theories and speculations.
