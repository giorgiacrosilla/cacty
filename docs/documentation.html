Documentation

The project is intended to present a magazine about the Secret Organizations, divided in three issues and enriched by metadata.

The roles inside the group have been divided as follows:

  • Giorgia Crosilla tagged the texts related to the KKK and developed the 90s and Dystopian Future styles;
  • Salvatore Di Marzo worked on the texts on Skull and Bones and developed the Aldine and De Stijl styles;
  • Nicole Liggeri worked on the Freemasonry issue and developed the 1860 and 70s styles.

1990s

Cioè magazine

Visual References

This style has been inspired by the “Cioè”, a cult magazine among Italian teenagers from the 80s to the beginning of the XXI century. Characterized basically by bad choices in design, random changes in fonts, colors and layout within the same page, looking at some pages of the Cioè Magazine today gives us chills but also a lot of nostalgia. In this style we have tried to take elements from some covers of the magazine and internal pages to reconstruct the website at its resemblance.

Layout

The two main concepts around which the layout of the magazine revolves around are bidimensionality and irregularity of shapes. This is translated on the website using triangular shapes as separators for the different sections of the main page, or the rotated buttons inside the sidebar. The text in the main page also follows a diagonal positioning. Each article page presents the text split into three columns. Very thick borders characterize the images that have been added, as for the borders of the table. As can be seen, the paragraphs are irregular: some have different background colors, other different border styles. The buttons inside the sidebar have been inspired by the titles of the cover page.

Font and color palette

We have extracted the three main colors of the cover magazine, and from there we have tried to build a homogeneous palette based on bright and mainly cold colors. In last two decades of the XX century, TrueType fonts were born to contain both information for the correct display on screen and on paper. In 1982 the famous Arial was born, a neo-grotesque font designed to be suitable for laser printers that combines the characteristics of Helvetica and Univers. Four fonts have been used in the development of the website:

  • “Lobster” to recreate the italic font of the logo of the original “Cioè” magazine.
  • “Telelower”, a font created in 1997 the Canadian cartoon channel “Teletoon”. Here it is used for titles and subititles used to convey a sense of irregularity and fun which transpares from the different fonts used in the original magazine.
  • “Coolvetica” is a funky variant of the Helvetica font, created in the 70s.
  • "Helvetica", a classic XX century font. Even though, the emblem of the fonts during the two last decades of the XX century was Arial, here we decided to stick with Helvetica, both because it was used in the magazine, along with Helvetica Compressed and also because it can be easily combined with its derivative “coolvetica”.

Dystopian future

Visual References

The main inpiration for the development of this style is the dystopian novel "1984" written by George Orwell in 1949. The author imagines a future governed by a tiranny in which every man on Earth is controlled constantly by "The Big Brother", usually represented as a red eye. The layout and choices that have been made for this style are inspired by both the 40s propaganda, future dystopia and designs for posters related to the book "1984".
